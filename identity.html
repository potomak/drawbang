identity

Drawbang has no accounts and no logins. Instead, the editor generates an Ed25519 keypair in your browser the first time you publish. The public half of that keypair becomes your handle: every drawing you sign with it shows up on a page at /keys/<your-public-key>.

Once a key exists in this browser, this nav link rewrites itself to profile and jumps straight to your owner page. If you're seeing the identity label and this explainer, no key has been generated yet — open the editor, draw something, and click publish to gallery to mint one.

good to know

  • The secret half of the key lives only on this device (IndexedDB). Lose the device, lose the key — there's no account-recovery flow.
  • You can export the key as a JWK file from the editor's settings dialog and re-import it on another device.
  • Drawings you made before you had a key are unattributed forever; the operator has occasionally backfilled missing signatures (see #82).
